## How MailValid works

MailValid runs every email address through a three-layer verification pipeline before returning a result.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Syntax validation">
    The email format is checked against RFC 5322 standards. Typos and malformed addresses are rejected immediately — before any network call is made.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Domain & MX check">
    MailValid looks up the domain and confirms it has valid mail exchange (MX) records configured. Domains with no DNS entries are flagged as invalid.
  </Step>
  <Step title="SMTP mailbox verification">
    MailValid performs an SMTP handshake with the mail server to confirm the specific mailbox exists — all without sending an actual email.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Every verification response includes more than a pass/fail result.

    Identifies addresses like info@, support@, or admin@ that represent departments, not individuals.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Catch-all flagging" icon="net">
    Marks domains that accept mail to any address, so you can decide your own risk tolerance.

    A 0–100 confidence score with machine-readable sub-status reasons for every result.
